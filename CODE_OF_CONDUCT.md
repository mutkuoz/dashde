# Code of conduct

We want dashde to be a project people enjoy contributing to. That means:

- **Be kind.** Assume good faith. People writing free code on the
  internet often have day jobs, caretaking responsibilities, and
  complicated lives. Patience is free.
- **Critique code, not people.** "This has a race condition" is fine.
  "You have no idea what you're doing" is not.
- **Don't be a dick about indentation.** Or themes, or naming, or
  whether tests belong in the same file.
- **No harassment.** No slurs. No sustained hostility. Maintainers will
  remove content and block offenders at their discretion, including for
  patterns of behavior across issues, PRs, and adjacent forums.

If you see someone being treated badly — including by a maintainer —
email the maintainer listed in `package.json`, or open an issue marked
`conduct`.

This applies to every space the project occupies: issues, PRs,
discussions, chat, video calls, conference booths.
